Eight years building in health tech, insurtech, and proptech — industries where regulatory constraints, messy data, and real human decisions all land in the same product. Most recently at Acrisure, and before that Rhino, where I led the launch of ancillary insurance products that reached meaningful revenue milestones.

I've also built outside the corporate track — co-founding Rapidocc, a healthcare SaaS company, and acquiring and exiting Sit Means Sit Austin, a dog training franchise that grew to $1.7M in revenue.

Outside of work I'm a parent of two in Austin, TX — which has taught me more about systems thinking, stakeholder negotiation, and shipping under pressure than any PM course ever could.

I've long been drawn to Stoic and Buddhist philosophy — less as doctrine and more as frameworks for navigating uncertainty. The idea that you can only control your response, not the outcome, has quietly shaped how I approach product work: stay process-oriented, hold results loosely, and keep showing up.
